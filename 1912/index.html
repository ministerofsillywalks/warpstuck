Now that you are no longer stuck at home, you find yourself on the streets of Williamstown. A harsh wind blows across the hills (although not as harsh as a few weeks ago). It carries a familiar note—the note a jaunty becapped flautist plays to keep his instrument in tune.

It is a WARP night, and as with many you have been a part of before it, something seems to be missing. In fact, you know something to be missing. Double banagrams, a game usually in the possession of WARP, has been gone from the cabinet for several weeks. This game, which is, at present, eluding you, is the latest geis in the hand of an unknown chieftain. His strategies are less of a Brenn than of a common warlord. His scheme is Absence itself, a mystery dispersing like 5 red fireworks into the night. It is the most diabolical game of all.

    “We don’t stop playing because we grow old, we grow old because we stop playing” -Mark Hopkins

Yes, you are certain that Mark Hopkins said that. One hundred percent positive.

You have a feeling it’s gonna be a long night
